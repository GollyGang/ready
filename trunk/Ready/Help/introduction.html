What is reaction-diffusion?

Alan Turing, after inventing the computer* and single-handedly winning the war*, published a paper called The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis in 1952. This showed how chemical morphogens could diffuse through living tissue and drive development in embryos and plants. The key result is that instabilities can arise and form static patterns.
